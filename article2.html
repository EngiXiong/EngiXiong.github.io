ASML's Story

America wants to undermine entire Chinese industries: the US has for years increasingly restricted China’s semiconductor exports. Companies like ASML, a Dutch semiconductor manufacturing company, get caught and suffer in the crossfire. For instance, in 2020 they pressured the Dutch government, due to national security concerns regarding China’s militarism, to ban the export of EUVs, something of which they are known for, However, in the process of cutting off a major customer, ASML will and has experienced considerable losses. According to ASML’s 2023 second-quarter financial report, it was revealed that China alone accounted for over 20% of their total earnings. What’s worse is that the US is demanding further restrictions on ASML to discontinue the sales of DUV equipment (less advanced lithography equipment) to China starting in 2024. If this were to happen, which it is very likely to be, given that ASML still has not yet renewed their licenses to trade with China for next year, ASML is estimated to suffer from over $2 billion dollars in lost revenue. A DUV sanction will be much more damaging for ASML than the ban on EUV states Wang Xialong, a research head at ICWise, a Shanghai semiconductor consultancy. Overall, ASML’s transports and profits are heavily constrained, and will continue to suffer more. For a company in this much relentlessly sanctioned-heated hot water, they’re limited from achieving their full potential and it makes them very unstable.

Although semiconductors are pivotal components for much of our widely-used technologies, making them reliable products—the companies that make them can change. Especially if their customer, who has the 2nd largest economy in the world and poised to become the 1st, becomes their competitor…(yes this is a foreshadow)
